# Technology Stack Selection Checklist

- State product constraints, hard disqualifiers, scale assumptions, team maturity, and operating model.
- Inventory approved in-house stacks and compare boring proven options before accepting novelty.
- Require at least two candidates and a weighted matrix whose weights come from product priorities.
- Review security posture, dependency surface, license fit, supply-chain posture, and ecosystem maturity.
- Check official support policy, EOL horizon, maintainer/vendor ownership, and upgrade cadence.
- Estimate 3-year TCO: maintenance, hiring/upskilling, upgrades, hosting, observability, on-call, and migration cost.
- Identify integration constraints, generated artifacts, package managers, CI/build/deploy lanes, and unsupported requirements.
- Classify reversibility, exit path, migration/coexistence window, rollback trigger, and re-evaluation date.
- Name owners for operation, upgrades, security patching, and incident response before approval.
- Record rejected alternatives with concrete tradeoffs, not preference language.
- Map accepted stack risks to validation: build, test, benchmark, security, observability, migration, rollback, or specialist gate.
- State evidence limits: what the decision proves, what it does not prove, stale evidence, and residual risk owner.
